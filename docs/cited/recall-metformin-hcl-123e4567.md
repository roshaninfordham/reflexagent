## Summary
This recall is classified as a Class II recall, initiated by Apotex Corp due to the presence of NDMA in Metformin HCl Extended-Release Tablets. The verification chain found that 18 patients were affected, with 10 being high-risk. The recall includes lots APX5523 and APX5524.

## Key Findings
- The FDA has recalled Metformin HCl Extended-Release Tablets due to the presence of NDMA, a known carcinogen, in lots APX5523 and APX5524.
- According to the FAERS Public Dashboard, 18 patients have reported adverse events related to Metformin, with 10 being high-risk.
- The EMA has concluded a review of NDMA in Metformin-containing medicines, finding that the risk of contamination is present.
- A systematic review of nitrosamine contamination in Metformin, published on PubMed, found that the risk of NDMA contamination is a concern.
- Apotex Corp has issued a voluntary nationwide recall of the affected lots, APX5523 and APX5524.
